# Scoring Loop

## Scoring Goal

This loop decides whether a script is formally usable or must go back for revision.

## Score Dimensions

Use a 100-point scale:

- opening stop power: 20
- clarity of one root problem: 15
- practicality of method and steps: 15
- proof anchor strength: 15
- follow reason strength: 15
- CTA stability and safety: 10
- real-person tone and AI boundary: 10

## Pass Line

A script is formally usable only if all of these are true:

- structure review: pass
- risk review: pass
- audience positive count: 8/12 or higher
- final score: 82/100 or higher

## Rewrite Logic

When failing, identify the smallest broken area first:

- wrong root problem
- weak hook
- weak proof anchor
- weak follow reason
- risky CTA
- AI boundary missing

## Rewrite Order

Fix in this order:

1. root problem
2. ending and follow clue
3. proof anchor
4. hook
5. step clarity

## Reviewer Output Format

- total score
- whether it passes
- top 3 reasons for failure
- exact lines to rewrite
- suggested replacement direction

## Practical Rule

Do not rewrite the whole script by default.

Only rewrite the smallest section that broke the pass line, then re-run review.
